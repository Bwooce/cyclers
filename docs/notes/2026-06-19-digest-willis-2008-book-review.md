## What it is
A two-page review of **"Modern Astrodynamics,"** ed. P. Gurfil — Elsevier
Astrodynamics Series, 1st ed., vol. 1, Academic Press (2006), 336 pp,
ISBN 978-0-12-373562-9. This is the SAME volume already deep-digested
under task #395 (`digest-gurfil-2007-modern-astrodynamics.md`); the review
is corroborating metadata, not a new primary source. Its only standalone
value is independently confirming the volume's chapter structure and
author set, and flagging which topics the wider community considered the
book's contribution.

## Contents reported by the review (p.230)
- **Contributing authors:** D.A. Vallado, M. Efroimsky, V.M. Guibout,
  D.J. Scheeres, E. Belbruno, M. Dellnitz, O. Junge, I.M. Ross,
  C.R. McInnes, M. Cartmell, L. Breger, G. Inalhan, M. Tillerson, J.P. How.
- **8 chapters** (as listed in the review): (1) perturbed motion;
  (2) gauge freedom in astrodynamics; (3) solving two-point boundary-value
  problems using generating functions; (4) low-energy transfers and
  applications; (5) set-oriented methods in space mission design;
  (6) space trajectory optimization and L¹-optimal control problems;
  (7) orbital mechanics of propellant-less propulsion systems;
  (8) cooperative spacecraft formation flying.
- Highlighted strengths (p.231): ballistic-capture transfers Earth→lunar
  orbit without retro-propulsion (Belbruno); low-energy orbits;
  Hamiltonian methods; per-chapter reference lists + worked demonstrations;
  solar sails (McInnes), tethers (Cartmell), formation flying.
