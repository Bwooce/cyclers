## Corpus errata uncovered by the read

The pre-#346 `KNOWN_CORPUS` entry had **three** mismatches against the
actual paper:

| Field | Pre-#346 | Actual | Severity |
|---|---|---|---|
| `name` / citation title | "Tulip-shaped orbits in the Saturn system" | "Trajectory design for Saturnian Ocean Worlds orbiters using multidimensional Poincaré maps" | shorthand, not actual title |
| `doi` | `10.1016/j.actaastro.2017.11.011` | `10.1016/j.actaastro.2017.11.004` | **wrong DOI digit** |
| `body_set` | `{Titan, Enceladus, Rhea, Dione}` | (paper documents) `{Titan, Enceladus}` only | **over-wide — over-captured #344** |

Tightening the body_set from `{Titan, Enceladus, Rhea, Dione}` to
`{Titan, Enceladus}` is the load-bearing correction. Reasoning:

* The literature-check matcher tests `seq_set <= anchor.body_set` (full
  subset). Pre-#346, any (Titan, Rhea) or (Titan, Dione) sequence matched
  the Davis anchor.
* The paper actually documents only Saturn-Titan TULIP orbits (Figs. 8-11,
  ~6:1 resonance with periapses centred over Titan's poles) and
  Saturn-Enceladus L1/L2 NRHO halo families + a heteroclinic transfer
  *Enceladus halo → Titan polar coverage orbit*.
* **Rhea and Dione are mentioned in the introduction's ocean-worlds context
  but are NOT orbit-family targets in any documented family in the paper.**
  No Titan-Rhea-Titan (1,1) repeated-moon cycler topology appears
  anywhere.

## Conservative scope of the corpus edit

I did NOT:

* Add a `period_band_tu` to the corrected anchor. The paper's tulip family
  is at Saturn-Titan ~6:1 resonance — that period in CR3BP TU could be
  bracketed sourced, but I do not have the unrounded value at hand from
  the read and prefer to leave the period filter unstated rather than
  put a derived value in a sourced anchor.
* Split into two anchors (`{Titan}` tulip + `{Enceladus}` NRHO). The
  paper documents both family types in the same publication; a single
  anchor with `body_set = {Titan, Enceladus}` continues to honour the
  publication-level granularity of `KNOWN_CORPUS` and correctly captures
  a future candidate visiting (Titan, Enceladus) (e.g. the paper's own
  heteroclinic transfer topology). Future evidence may justify a split,
  but #346's scope is correctness, not granularity.
