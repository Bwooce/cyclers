## 2. What the paper actually is

A **conceptual architecture and habitation study** for a SpaceX-Mars-colonization-scale cycler vehicle, framed around Musk's 1M-settlers-by-2050 / 1000-Starships-per-launch-opportunity goal. The paper proposes constructing a toroidal artificial-gravity cycler vehicle in LEO using the Offworld Industries "Sargon" cassette-assembler robotic construction system (Figure 1), then boosting it into a generic "Mars Cycler" orbit reusing the Rauwolf-Friedlander-Nock 2002 "up escalator / down escalator" pair (Figure 3). Mass & Equipment Lists (MELs) for both a minimal-sized (~60 persons) and cruise ship-sized (~1,000 persons) cycler are tabulated.

Six sections plus references:

1. **Introduction** (p.2): scene-setting; cites Boyle 2016 / Kooser 2020 / SpaceX 2024 / Drake 2009 / Connolly et al 2017 / Zubrin 2011 etc.; positions the problem of moving 100+ persons per vehicle for six months.
2. **Back Up a Bit: Large-scale Space Construction** (pp.2-3): Sargon panel-cassette robotic assembler concept (Figures 1, 2). 2.5m × 2.5m panels welded into rings (8 cassettes → 20m circumference cylinder, 12 cassettes → 30m).
3. **Earth-Mars Transit: A Mars Cycler** (p.4): brief astrodynamics overview citing Aldrin/Byrnes-Longuski-Aldrin 1993 / Hollister 1969 / Chen et al 2012 / Rogers-Hughes-Aldrin 2015 / Landau-Longuski 2007. **Figure 3 is a redrawn version of Rauwolf-Friedlander-Nock 2002 up/down escalator pair.** ΔV calculation for LEO insertion: 4,739 m/s assuming v_inf = 6,000 m/s.
4. **Minimal-sized Artificial Gravity Mars Cycler** (pp.5-10): 60-person torus design. Figures 4-11 = MEL tables (control panel, AG parameters, propellant, structures, spacecraft subsystems, habitat functions, laboratory functions, consumables). Total mass 1,201,280 kg. 25m radius rotating inner torus at 5.92 rpm tangential 15.81 m/s. Outer non-rotating torus + counter-rotating flywheel for angular-momentum cancellation.
5. **Scaling Up: A Cruise Ship-sized Mars Cycler** (pp.11-12): 1,005-person torus. Total mass 6,572,059 kg. Figures 12-15 = larger MELs and cross-section views.
6. **Conclusion** (pp.13): "Once the Mars Cycler has been placed in its cycler orbit, it will continue the trajectory indefinitely with very little propulsive boost required."

## 3. Per-encounter / per-cycler data extraction — NEGATIVE RESULT

### 3.1 Total V_∞ data in the paper

**Exactly one V_∞ value appears in the paper**, on p.4:

> "Δv = √(v_inf² + 2GM/r) − √(GM/r) = 4,739 m/s
> Assumes that v-infinity = 6,000 m/s (Rauwolf, et al 2002), Earth GM = 3.986×10¹⁴ m³/s², Earth radius = 6,378,000 m, and LEO construction orbit altitude is 300,000 m."

This is the LEO injection ΔV assuming Rauwolf 2002's v_inf = 6 km/s departure. **It is not a per-encounter V_∞**. The "cycler" trajectory in Figure 3 is a generic up/down escalator schematic with no V_∞ values at Earth or Mars encounters — only "Day 0", "Day 151", "Day 59", "Day 229" labels indicating example duration.

### 3.2 Trajectory data published (Figure 3, p.4)

| Encounter | Day | Body | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Up escalator depart | 0 | Earth | "Earth 1" in figure |
| Up escalator arrive | 151 | Mars | "Mars 1" — 151-day Earth→Mars transit |

**These four data points are reproduced from Rauwolf-Friedlander-Nock 2002**, not original to this paper. The paper explicitly says: "In particular Rauwolf, Friedlander, and Nock (2002) elaborate on 'up and down escalators' where two cyclers with mirroring trajectories allow for periodic transits for less than 180 days (six months)." (p.4)

### 3.3 Mission profile constants (Figure 4 MEL control panel, p.5)

- **Mission duration**: 181 days (one-way Earth-Mars transit)
- **Trajectory ΔV** (LEO insertion): 5,000 m/s
- **Mission type**: T = Transit
- **Max distance from Sun**: 1.52 AU (Mars)
- **Cabin pressure**: 15 psi
- **Crew (minimal)**: 61 persons
- **Crew (cruise-ship)**: 1,005 persons
- **Artificial gravity**: 1.00 g at outer rim
- **Rotation**: 5.92 rpm (minimal), 4.62 rpm (cruise-ship)
- **Tangential velocity**: 15.81 m/s (minimal), 20.29 m/s (cruise-ship)

### 3.4 Mass & Equipment List highlights

**Minimal-sized cycler** (61 persons, 181-day mission):
- Total mass loaded: 1,201,280 kg
- Pressurized volume: 4,227 m³ (~50 m³/person + 10 m³/person habitable)
- Inner torus mass: 272,130 kg
- Counter-rotating flywheel: 716,743 kg
- Outer torus (non-rotating): 209,069 kg
- Elevator mass: 3,338 kg
- Propellant for LEO insertion: oxidizer (N₂O₄) 4,727 m³ in 21m sphere; fuel (MMH) 4,575 m³ in 21m sphere
- Total propellant tank: 73 Starship-2 (150t) launches OR 55 Starship-3 (200t) launches to refuel

**Cruise ship-sized cycler** (1,005 persons, 181-day mission):
- Total mass loaded: 6,572,059 kg
- Pressurized volume: 50,419 m³ (~50 m³/person)
- Total power: 11,603 kW
- Construction launches: 44 Starship-2 OR 7 Starship-3 (construction) + 395 Starship-2 OR 296 Starship-3 (propellant refueling)

### 3.5 Propulsion and chemistry choices

- Chemical: N₂O₄ oxidizer + Monomethyl Hydrazine (MMH) fuel. Acknowledged "in reality, 50-70 Starship launches carrying toxic propellant may pose a significant environmental risk."
- Once in cycler orbit: SEP (Solar Electric Propulsion) for trajectory maintenance, "current technology" but not designed in this paper.
- For the "passive cycler" phase between encounters: no additional propulsion needed (per the Rauwolf assumption of 1,500 m/s every 15 years to maintain cycler orbit using SEP at Isp = 5,000s).

## 6. Errata vs prior assumptions

1. **#115 was mis-scoped.** The blocker "no published V_∞" was correct, but the inference that Howe 2025 was an astrodynamics paper to be unblocked once V_∞ surfaced was incorrect. Howe 2025 is structurally an architecture paper that will never publish original cycler V_∞.
2. **No new "escalator" cycler is introduced.** The up/down escalator pair is Rauwolf-Friedlander-Nock 2002's. The catalogue must trace the citation chain backward, not forward.
3. **Mars Cycler corpus completeness check**: the Howe 2025 reference list is the cleanest single Mars-cycler review available in the corpus — it captures the canonical chain (Hollister 1969 → Aldrin 1985 → Byrnes-Longuski-Aldrin 1993 → Niehoff/Friedlander VISIT → Rauwolf 2002 → Chen 2012 → Rogers-Hughes-Aldrin 2015 → Landau-Longuski 2007) in ~25 entries. **Useful as a corpus-completeness checklist.**
4. **The trajectory ΔV = 5,000 m/s LEO-to-cycler assumption (p.5) is somewhat high.** For the Aldrin 1L1 cycler (V_∞_E = 6.54 km/s per McConaghy 2002 Table 4), the Earth departure ΔV from 300km LEO is √(6540² + 2·3.986e14/6.678e6) − √(3.986e14/6.678e6) ≈ √(42.8e6 + 119e6) − 7726 ≈ 12722 − 7726 ≈ 5000 m/s. So 5 km/s is roughly Aldrin-cycler-magnitude. For Rauwolf's lower 6 km/s assumption, ΔV ≈ 4,739 m/s as the paper states. **The numbers check out** under the v_inf assumption — but the assumption itself is a free parameter.
5. **The 1,500 m/s per 15 years SEP maintenance figure (p.4)** is attributed to Rauwolf 2002 — should be verified against AIAA 2002-5046 once acquired.
