## What it is (the architecture — load-bearing for our classifications)
Free-return cyclers that "repeatedly shuttle a spacecraft between two bodies using little or no
fuel," generalised from interplanetary cyclers to **intermoon** cyclers. The **enumerative**
ideal-model search (circular-coplanar moons) finds "**hundreds of ideal model ballistic cycler
geometries**"; several representative cases are documented; many transition accurately to real
ephemerides.

**CRITICAL — the census is NOT "any two moons" (corrects a 2026-06-30 over-claim of mine).**
Table 1 ("Planetary moon ideal models considered") lists the SPECIFIC (free-return-flyby-body →
target-body) systems actually enumerated:
- **Jovian:** Ganymede→Io, Ganymede→Europa, Ganymede→Callisto, Europa→Ganymede (the flyby body
  is the big moon; the target is the other Galilean).
- **Saturnian:** **Titan→Enceladus ONLY.** Titan is the flyby body because it is "the largest
  moon in the Saturn system by almost 2 orders of magnitude" and the only one massive enough to
  provide the gravity assists; the icy moons (Enceladus, Rhea, Dione, Tethys, Mimas) are
  TARGETS, not flyby bodies.
- (Heliocentric calibration: EM, EV, VM, V-Mercury; a few new ones recorded in the paper's [26].)

So the "hundreds of geometries" are hundreds of **Titan-Enceladus** (and the listed Jovian-pair)
geometries — different resonances/itineraries of those pairs — NOT a census across all moon pairs.
