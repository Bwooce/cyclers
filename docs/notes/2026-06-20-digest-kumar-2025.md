### Heteroclinic Metric and Connections
- **Perigee Poincaré Section:** Defined at osculating true and mean anomaly zero (perilune passages where $\dot{r} \cdot r = 0$).
- **Distance Metric (Eq 10, 11):** 
  $D(b, c) = \sqrt{||L_b - L_c||^2 + ||A_b - A_c||^2}$
  where $L$ is the specific angular momentum vector and $A$ is the Laplace vector in the Earth-centered inertial frame.
- **3:1 to 2:1 Heteroclinics (Table 1):**
  - Exist for $C_J \le 3.15$
  - For $C_J = 3.00$: Type 1 (short) transfer time $\approx 30.415$ days
  - For $C_J = 3.05$: Type 1 (short) transfer time $\approx 32.895$ days
  - For $C_J = 3.10$: Type 2 (long) transfer time $\approx 60.212$ days
  - For $C_J = 3.15$: Type 2 (long) transfer time $\approx 61.420$ days
- Note that the 3:1 -> 2:1 chain Candidate is present for common Jacobi values up to 3.15.

### Heteroclinic Candidates (3:1 $\rightarrow$ 2:1) Table 5 Intersections
The paper lists specific $x, y, \dot{x}, \dot{y}$ for exact manifold intersections (e.g. Type 1 at 3.00, 3.05 and Type 2 at 3.10, 3.15).

## Transport Barrier (4:1 $\rightarrow$ 3:1)
The paper demonstrates that no natural heteroclinic connections exist from the 4:1 to the 3:1 resonance at any energy level due to rotational invariant circles (1D KAM tori) preventing intersection of the invariant manifolds.
