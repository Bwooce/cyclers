## Scope

Quantifies **resonance widths** (stable regions) and **chaotic-zone extents** (unstable regions) for
the Earth-Moon **2:1 and 3:1 MMRs**, using the restricted three-body problem:

- Resonance zones rigorously defined via the **separatrix of unstable resonant periodic orbits**
  surrounding stable quasi-periodic regions (same separatrix-based definition used across this
  paper cluster).
- Uses a **perigee Poincaré map**, results presented in geocentric orbital elements (semimajor axis
  vs. eccentricity plane).
- Key finding (per abstract): the actual regions of resonance influence are **broader than predicted
  by semi-analytical models** — i.e. classical analytic resonance-width estimates (Chirikov-type)
  understate the true chaotic-zone extent found by the full numerical CR3BP treatment, especially at
  small Jacobi constant (high energy).

## Relevance to this project

A quantitative companion to the two structural papers above — this one gives the actual **width/extent
numbers** for the 2:1 and 3:1 resonance zones the other papers describe structurally. Relevant to any
future work computing MMR-based transport budgets or chaotic-zone escape times in the Earth-Moon
system (#411/#496/#503 territory). The "broader than semi-analytical prediction" finding is a useful
sourced caveat if this project ever cites a Chirikov-style resonance-width estimate for cislunar work.
