## What the paper actually is

A relay-spacecraft trajectory-design study for **continuous Earth-Mars
communication** during the periods of Earth-Mars solar conjunction (which
occur every 26 months and degrade RF/optical comms for 17-90 days at a
time). The paper asks: *what orbit family lets a single relay spacecraft
stay in the "comm zone" (outside the solar exclusion cone, within Mars's
communication sphere) continuously, without stationkeeping, for decades?*

Method:

1. **Geometric constraint derivation** (Eqs. 1-11): for a minimum
   sun-Earth-Mars angle Φ, the minimum spacecraft-Mars distance d is a
   function of Mars's heliocentric radius — see Fig. 4. For Φ ≤ 15°, d ≤
   1.2 AU.
2. **Orbit-family screening** (Table 2): every periodic orbit family in
   JPL's Monte / CRTBP periodic-orbit database is screened against the
   geometric and temporal constraints. Sun-Mars families evaluated: L1-L5
   axial/halo/Lyapunov/butterfly/vertical/short/long families, Distant
   Retrograde, Distant Prograde, Low Prograde, Dragonfly, Resonant 1:1
   through 3:4.
3. **Result:** **only the L4 and L5 LONG-PERIOD families** in the Sun-Mars
   (and Sun-Earth) CRTBPs satisfy both the *exterior to solar exclusion
   cone* and *remains in comm zone for extended period* constraints.
4. **L4/L5 long-period orbit dynamics:** ~1240-year orbital period (in
   Sun-Mars system, mass ratio µ ≈ 3.2×10⁻⁷); orbit circumnavigates one
   triangular equilibrium point. Truly periodic only in CRTBP; in the
   ephemeris model the orbits become **Trojan orbits** (quasi-periodic
   counterparts).

Quantitative results:

- **Direct transfer** (Lambert from Earth to Mars-Trojan-insertion point):
  example transfer Sep 2022 dep → Aug 2023 arr, 334 days flight, v_∞ =
  4.24 km/s, orbit-insertion Δv = 2174 m/s (multibody-optimized; the
  unconstrained-final-state version is 2408 m/s for full state match).
- **Outbound-flyby transfer** (Earth → Mars flyby → phasing orbit → Trojan
  insertion): example Nov 2022 dep → May 2023 flyby → Mar 2025 insertion,
  855 days, v_∞ = 8.50 km/s, **Δv₂ = 604 m/s** (vs 2174 m/s direct — 72%
  savings).
- Launch-opportunity scan (Fig. 19): 2022, 2024, 2026, 2028 minimum-total-Δv
  for 45-day-lead Trojans: **750, 610, 580, 840 m/s** respectively, with
  C₃ ≤ 9-14 km²/s².
- **Trojan stability** (Fig. 11): ≤ 30° max sun-Mars-spacecraft drift over
  one Earth-Mars-Jupiter synodic period (47 yr) for 10-50 day initial
  Mars offsets — viable for decades without stationkeeping.

Earth-Mars solar conjunctions through 2100 tabulated (Table 1, 33 rows).
Important Sun-Mars L4/L5 Trojan asteroids referenced: Mikkola-Innanen 1994
(stability), Connors 2005, Scholl-Marzari-Tricarico 2005 (dynamics of
Mars Trojans), Rivkin et al 2007 (L5 Mars Trojans composition).
