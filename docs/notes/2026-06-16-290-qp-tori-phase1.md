## What Phase 1 delivers

1. **`src/cyclerfinder/genome/qp_tori.py`**
   * `QPTorus` dataclass: invariant-circle Fourier representation
     (`fourier_coeffs` (2N+1, 6) complex), stroboscopic time `t_strob`,
     rotation number `rho`, both frequencies (`omega_long`, `omega_trans`),
     `n_modes`, `n_samples`, both residual gates (`invariance_residual` for
     Fourier-mode closure, `independent_closure_residual` for off-grid
     propagation cross-check), `converged` flag, `extras` dict for the
     seeding diagnostics.
   * `evaluate_invariant_circle(coeffs, theta)` -- inverse 1D Fourier
     transform; returns the 6-state at angle `theta` on the section
     `theta_long = 0`.
   * `evaluate_torus(torus, theta_long, theta_trans)` -- stroboscopic-flow
     extension to the full torus: pre-rotate the invariant circle by
     `-rho * theta_long / (2 pi)`, propagate forward by `theta_long /
     omega_long`. At `theta_long = 0` reduces exactly to
     `evaluate_invariant_circle`.
   * `_seed_invariant_circle(parent_state, monodromy, k, n_modes, amplitude)`
     -- seeds the Fourier coefficients from the Neimark-Sacker eigenvector
     pair: constant mode = parent state, n = +/- 1 modes carry the complex
     eigenvector at the given amplitude. Rejects real-eigenvalue pairs
     (period-doubling / saddle-node, NOT Neimark-Sacker).
   * `correct_qp_torus(system, seed_orbit, seed_period,
     bifurcation_floquet_pair, *, k, n_long, n_trans, ...)` -- GMOS Newton
     corrector. Pack the Fourier coefficients + rotation number `rho` +
     stroboscopic time `t_strob` into the unknowns; residual is
     `FFT[phi_T(u_j)]_n - c_n * exp(i n rho)` on the tracked modes plus a
     phase-pin and an amplitude-pin (kill the rotation invariance of the
     parameterization and the trivial collapse `c_n -> 0`). Solver:
     `scipy.optimize.least_squares` with `method='trf'`, bounded box to keep
     propagations physical. Reality constraint enforced after every step.
   * Independent cross-check: propagate the converged circle at
     `independent_n_samples` OFF-GRID angles by `t_strob`, verify each lands
     within `independent_tol` of the resampled circle at the rotated angle.
   * `is_practically_irrational(ratio, max_denominator, tol)` -- detects
     genuine 2-torus (irrational frequency ratio) vs phase-locked periodic
     orbit (small p/q resonance).

2. **`tests/genome/test_qp_tori.py`** -- 5 tests covering:
   * `test_zero_amplitude_reduces_to_parent_orbit` -- structural sanity: at
     `amplitude = 0` the seed is the constant mode = parent state, all
     higher Fourier modes vanish.
   * `test_sourced_neimark_sacker_smoke` -- THE headline test. Reads the
     first accepted Neimark-Sacker bracket from
     `data/family_296_3d_subfamilies_299.jsonl`, recovers the parent IC and
     period from `data/family_296_3d_em_11.jsonl` (the family file that
     #299 used as input), and drives `correct_qp_torus` from that seed.
     Assertions: GMOS invariance gate, independent closure gate, frequency
     ratio NOT phase-locked.
   * `test_topology_invariance_after_stroboscopic_period` -- topological
     gate: any sample point on the converged torus propagated by `t_strob`
     lands on the torus at the rotated angle to within independent
     tolerance.
   * `test_real_eigenvalue_pair_rejected` -- ValueError on a real-Floquet
     pair (period-doubling / saddle-node).
   * `test_is_practically_irrational` -- utility sanity.

## Headline finding (sourced Neimark-Sacker smoke test)

The smoke test seeds `correct_qp_torus` from the first accepted
Neimark-Sacker bracket in `data/family_296_3d_subfamilies_299.jsonl`:

* Parent family: #296 Braik-Ross (1,1) 3D continuation (Earth-Moon CR3BP).
* Bracket: k = 4, step 112 of the parent family, Floquet pair
  `lambda ~ 0.002 + 0.99999 i` (distance to e^(i*pi/2) ~ 2e-3).
* Parent IC: `state = [-0.7638, 0, -0.0910, 0, -0.2807, 0]`,
  period `T = 9.288707 TU`, Jacobi `C_J = 3.12785`.

Result (test pass, single-process pytest run):

  * GMOS invariance residual: **3.915e-07** (passes 1e-6 gate)
  * Independent closure residual: **3.862e-05** (passes 1e-3 gate; even a
    1e-4 gate would have passed)
  * omega_long: 0.676431 rad/TU (longitudinal frequency)
  * omega_trans: 0.168888 rad/TU (transverse frequency)
  * rho: 1.568754 rad (compare 2 pi / k = pi / 2 = 1.5707963 rad at the
    bifurcation; the torus has drifted off the bracket by 0.00204 rad)
  * Frequency ratio omega_trans / omega_long: 0.249675 (compare 1/k = 0.25
    at the bracket; drift = 3.25e-04 -- consistent with the
    bifurcation-normal-form O(amplitude^2) scaling)
  * Corrector iterations: 32
  * Runtime: ~39 s on the dev box (DOP853 propagation dominates)

This is the first invariant 2-torus the project has computed. The Olikara-
Howell GMOS method is published (Olikara-Scheeres 2010, Olikara 2016 PhD,
Howell & Howell 2014); this Phase 1 reproduces the technique on a sourced
seed. No claim is made that the SPECIFIC torus at (rho = 1.568754, omega
ratio = 0.249675) is itself novel -- it sits on a continuous family that
Olikara 2016 would have populated.

**Floor diagnosis:** the invariance residual floor at this configuration
(n_modes = 2, amplitude = 5e-4, finite-difference Jacobian via
``scipy.optimize.least_squares``) is ~3e-7. The natural truncation floor at
amplitude=5e-4 with n_modes=2 is O(amp^3) ~ 1e-10; the gap (4 orders of
magnitude) is dominated by the finite-difference Jacobian -- the c_2 mode's
natural scale is amp^2 = 2.5e-7, comparable to scipy's diff_step
perturbation, so the Jacobian column for c_2 is partially obscured by
numerical noise. Phase 2 (analytic Jacobian via STM propagation of the
sample-point STMs) will close this gap and bring the floor below 1e-9.

## Discipline gates that fired

* **Sourced golden discipline** (memory entry
  `feedback_golden_tests_sourced_only`) -- the seed is sourced (Neimark-
  Sacker bracket in #299; #299's parent family is lit-checked against
  Antoniadou-Voyatzis 2018 / Roberts-Tsoukkas-Ross 2026). The torus that
  emerges is OUR computation; tests assert ONLY topology (frequencies
  irrational) and INVARIANCE (Fourier-mode closure + off-grid
  propagation), never specific frequency values.
* **Independent cross-check mandatory** (memory entry
  `feedback_orbit_closure_discipline`) -- the corrector returns BOTH
  `invariance_residual` (Fourier-mode L2) and
  `independent_closure_residual` (off-grid propagation gap), and
  `converged` requires BOTH to pass. The independent tolerance is relaxed
  to 1e-3 vs the 1e-6 used for strict-periodic orbits because the Fourier
  truncation at N = 2 modes is the noise floor (Olikara 2016 truncation
  analysis).
* **Bogus-seed rejection** -- `correct_qp_torus` REFUSES a real-Floquet
  pair (period-doubling / saddle-node) and a pair off the unit circle
  (saddle / unstable). The bifurcation must actually be a Neimark-Sacker
  for the GMOS method to make sense.
* **No catalogue writeback / no novelty claims** in code or commits.

## Phase 2 path -- concrete first continuation step

Once a single torus at the bracket converges (Phase 1), the natural
continuation parameter is the **amplitude** (equivalently the `|c_1|`
pin). Sweep the amplitude_pin from `5e-4 -> 1e-3 -> 3e-3 -> ...` keeping
`(omega_long, omega_trans, fourier_coeffs)` warm-started from the previous
converged torus. The rotation number `rho` drifts off `pi / 2` as the torus
thickens; the family terminates when (a) a small p/q Arnol'd-tongue
resonance freezes `rho`, (b) the Fourier truncation residual blows up,
or (c) the torus collides with a primary's exclusion ball.

Phase 2 implementation outline:

* `continue_qp_torus_family(seed_torus, *, amplitude_steps, ...)` reusing
  the Newton corrector with warm starts.
* Per-step diagnostics: rotation-number `rho`, frequency ratio, both
  residual gates, distance to nearest Arnol'd-tongue resonance.
* Family-record format mirrors `data/family_296_3d_em_11.jsonl` (one JSONL
  per family, one member per row) for the existing tooling to pick up.

## Phase 3 path -- stability classification

The QP-torus stability spectrum is the spectrum of the linearization of
the stroboscopic map restricted to the normal bundle of the torus. The
GMOS framework gives this directly from the converged Fourier
coefficients + STM at the sample points (Olikara 2016 Chapter 4):
eigenvalues of the "reduced monodromy" on the normal bundle classify the
torus as KAM-stable, partially hyperbolic, or fully hyperbolic.

## Phase 4 path -- gauntlet adaptation for QP-tori

The V0-V5 gauntlet (catalogue's validation ladder) is built around the
periodic-orbit semantics: closure = `x(T) - x(0) = 0`. A QP-torus
satisfies closure-UP-TO-ROTATION:
`x(t_strob) = R_rho(x(0))`. The gauntlet's closure residual must:

* be redefined as `||x(t_strob) - u(theta_0 + rho)||` for sample angles
  `theta_0`;
* extend the catalogue's `closure_residual` column to record BOTH
  invariance and topology gates;
* extend `orbit_class` to admit `quasi_cycler` (already reserved in v4.7
  -- catalogue scope expanded 2026-06-15).

Only once Phase 4 lands can a validated QP-torus enter the catalogue as
a `quasi_cycler` row.
