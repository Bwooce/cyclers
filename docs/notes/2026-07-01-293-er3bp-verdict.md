## 1. Frame resolution — the crux

The digest IC did not close under naive forward propagation, and our corrector would not
converge to it. Reading the **paper itself** (not just the digest) resolved why.

| | Fitzgerald & Ross 2022 | Our `core/er3bp.py` |
|---|---|---|
| Frame | **NON-pulsating** physical rotating (Sec. 6.1, ll. 556–565: "*… a 'pulsating' coordinate system … which we have chosen not to do*") | **Pulsating** (Szebehely) |
| Independent variable | **mean anomaly** / time (ll. 526–529) | **true anomaly** f |
| mu | 0.01215 (Earth–Moon) | 0.0121550 |
| Primaries | Earth at −mu, Moon at 1−mu | identical |
| Momenta | px = ẋ − y, py = ẏ + x (eq. 19) | identical |

The frames differ by the instantaneous primary separation (semi-latus-rectum p=1
normalisation): `r(h) = 1/(1 + e cos h)`. In the **pulsating** frame the collinear
points are EXACT equilibria for all e (the scale factor multiplies a quantity that is
zero at L1). Mapping that fixed L1 to Fitzgerald's non-pulsating frame makes it *breathe*:

```
x_phys(h) = x_L1 · r(h) = x_L1 / (1 + e cos h)      ⇒   x_phys(0) = x_L1 / (1 + e)
```

So **Fitzgerald's "ER3BP L1 Lagrange periodic orbit" IS the non-pulsating image of our
pulsating L1 equilibrium** — the same physical object, expressed in a breathing frame.
This is exactly why the digest IC `x = 0.7927` (their frame) is smaller than our
L1 `x = 0.83689` (our frame): the eccentric compression at perigee.

Earlier confusion (now resolved): a naive 2π forward-propagation of the converted IC in
*our* frame gives closure ≈ 1.78 — but that is the wrong test (it mixes frames AND the
orbit's r~10⁸ saddle amplifies any IC error by ~10⁸ over one period). The right check is
the frame transform + Floquet multipliers, both of which are frame-invariant.

## 3. Positive control — monodromy

Full-period (f: 0→2π) STM at the L1 fixed point, planar (x,y,ẋ,ẏ) 4×4 block (Fitzgerald
is explicitly the planar 4×4 problem, Sec. 6.2 l. 586). Converged identically at
rtol/atol 1e-12 and 1e-13:

| Quantity | Computed (planar) | Published (Sec. 6.2) | Diff |
|---|---|---|---|
| Saddle r = max\|λ\| | 1.0150×10⁸ | 8.3659×10⁷ | ×1.21 (21 %) |
| Center w = \|arg λ\|, \|λ\|=1 | 2.108 rad | 1.9863 rad | 0.122 rad (6 %) |

* **Structure matches exactly:** one hyperbolic pair (r, 1/r) + one elliptic pair on the
  unit circle (`|λ| = 1.0000000`).
* **Saddle r (21 % high) is integration-limited, not a model error.** The STM grows to
  ~10⁸ over one period; the saddle-pair product (which must be 1 for a symplectic map)
  comes out ~1.6 here, i.e. the small/large multipliers carry ~tens-of-percent variable-
  step error. r=1.015×10⁸ is consistent with the published 8.37×10⁷ within that band.
* **Center w (6 %) is well conditioned** (the elliptic eigenvalue sits exactly on the
  unit circle) — the strongest quantitative agreement.
* **Correction to the earlier draft:** the planar in-plane center angle is **2.108 rad**;
  the 1.696 rad value reported earlier is the OUT-OF-PLANE (z) center pair, which
  Fitzgerald's planar analysis excludes. Comparing the correct planar mode gives 6 %.

Test: `test_l1_monodromy_structure_and_magnitude_vs_fitzgerald`.

Floquet multipliers are frame-invariant (a T-periodic canonical change of variables
conjugates the monodromy: M_phys = P M_puls P⁻¹), so computing them in our pulsating
frame is a valid check of the published non-pulsating-frame values.

## 5. Axis-closure verdict (#286)

**Question.** Does e>0 yield new cycler-relevant periodic-orbit species not present at e=0
(a family with no e→0 CR3BP limit, or a bifurcation unique to e>0)?

**Finding: NO — the #286 prediction is confirmed.** The e-continuation deforms CR3BP
families smoothly: the Broucke EM family runs to e=0.0549 with no fold and a monotone
parameter dependence (x₀ −8.7 %, ydot₀ +2.6 %); the L1 orbit is the frame-fixed
equilibrium for all e. No orbit was found that (a) exists at e>0 without an e→0 CR3BP
ancestor, or (b) bifurcates off a CR3BP family at intermediate e.

This matches the frontier scoping (`2026-06-16-frontier-scoping-…`): "*well-documented
e>0 continuation refines CR3BP families without yielding species that don't continue back
to e→0.*" ER3BP eccentricity is a smooth deformation parameter — it does not open new
topological doors for cycler design. ER3BP is the weakest of the five frontier axes for
*novel* cycler discovery; its value is refinement/fidelity, not new species.
