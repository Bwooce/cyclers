## 2. What the paper actually is

The **establishment-cycler** paper — addresses how to insert a cycler vehicle
onto a cycler trajectory from LEO. **This is the `precursor_mga` class** in
the v4.7 catalogue scope expansion (per memory
`project_catalogue_scope_expanded_2026-06-15`).

Two insertion strategies compared:
1. **V∞ leveraging**: small deep-space Δv at first aphelion + tangential
   launch Δv → reduces total Δv vs direct-launch.
2. **Low-thrust spiral** + interplanetary low-thrust shaping: spiral out from
   LEO via SEP, then continuous-thrust interplanetary phase.

Eight cycler families considered: **Aldrin, VISIT-1, VISIT-2, Case 1, Case 2,
Case 3, S1L1, U0L1**.

## 3. Key numerical / structural content

### Methodology (§2, p.115-117)

**V∞ leveraging (§2.1)**: "the well-known use of a small deep-space maneuver
to modify the V∞ at a body." The technique is `K:L(M)±` per Sims-Longuski [21]:
- K = number of Earth orbit revolutions.
- L = number of spacecraft orbit revolutions.
- M = spacecraft orbit revolution on which the maneuver performed.
- ± = encounter performed after (+) or before (−) line of apsides.

Genetic algorithm (Eq 1a-1f, p.116): minimize J = ΣΔv = Δv_launch + Δv_aphelion
subject to |a_desired − a_genetic| ≤ a_error and |e_desired − e_genetic| ≤
e_error, with K, L, M ∈ ℤ and V∞, h ∈ ℝ. Tolerances: a_error = 0.09 AU,
e_error = 0.009.

K, L allowed 1-15; with V∞ above nominal between 1 and 3 km/s (1 m/s
precision), flyby altitude 300-5000 km (1 km precision) → ≈4.76 × 10¹⁰
possible initial conditions (p.116).

**Low-thrust analysis (§2.2)**: uses JPL's **MALTO** (Mission Analysis
Low-Thrust Optimization, ref [28]); V∞ at launch reduced to 0 km/s; multiple
Earth encounters during establishment phase, all reduced to V∞=0.
Spiral types: **circular** (Eq 2a-c) and **elliptical** (Eq 4a-d).

## 6. Errata / surprises

1. **The paper is 2015, not 2012**. The catalogue cites "Rogers et al. 2012";
   that's the conference precursor. The Acta Astro 2015 paper is the archival
   version with **richer data**. Add the 2015 DOI to citations.
2. **S1L1 in Rogers Table 1 has a=1.30, e=0.257, aphelion=1.64 AU**. But
   McConaghy 2006 Table 1 says leg 1 a=1.3039 AU (1.4889 yr period) and leg 2
   a=1.0483 AU. **Rogers is reporting LEG-1 values only** — consistent with
   "to establish the cycler vehicle, only the characteristics of the first leg
   are used" (p.118). This is a deliberate simplification, NOT an error.
3. **The 4:3(2)− and 3:2(1)− K:L(M)± nomenclature** is from Sims-Longuski
   1994 [18] and Capagnola-Russell 2010 [20], NOT this paper. The notation
   tells you how to do V∞ leveraging for any cycler.
4. **Rogers Table 1 lists "U0L1" as a cycler family** with full elements. U0L1
   is the McConaghy nPr n=0 unique-period / 1-rev case. **The `0` in `U0L1`
   means zero spacecraft revs** (Table 1 of McConaghy 2004) — a degenerate /
   special case. Catalogue should have a row for U0L1 if not present.
5. **The propellant savings from V∞-leveraging are MODEST for most cyclers
   except U0L1**:
   - Aldrin 4:3(2)−: 7 mt saved.
   - U0L1 3:2(1)−: **49 mt saved** (~25% of direct).
   The largest-V∞-flyby cycler benefits MOST from V∞-leveraging — a
   structural insight worth catalogue documentation.
6. **Low-thrust dominates V∞-leveraging by ~5x propellant mass reduction**
   (Aldrin: 7.7 mt low-thrust vs 126.4 mt V∞-leveraging). The paper concludes
   (p.124): **"low-thrust establishment should be used"** when large-scale
   SEP becomes available.
7. **The S1L1 launch date in Table 4 (12/20/2022)** is EARLIER than the
   McConaghy 2006 Table 2 Vehicle 2 launch date (12/03/2009 — ~13 years
   earlier). They're different launch windows for the same cycler family.
8. **The aldrin-cycler a=1.60, e=0.393, aphelion=2.23, perihelion=0.97 in
   Table 1 is the THIRD independent confirmation** of these values across
   Byrnes 1993, McConaghy 2004 (Table 4), and this paper. The catalogue's
   alternative a=1.659 / e=0.41 from spec.md is now THOROUGHLY contradicted
   by three peer-reviewed sources. **The 1.659 value should be retired** as
   the catalogue-preferred value.
