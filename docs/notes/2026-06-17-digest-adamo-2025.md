## What the paper actually is

A 23-slide LnL talk laying out Adamo's "waypoint" (WP) architecture for
spanning the Earth-Mars chasm, framed as a critique of NASA's Moon-to-Mars
(M2M) roadmap (which makes the Moon "appear halfway to Mars" when Mars is
actually 148 to 1038 lunar distances from Earth).

The architectural proposal: place **synodic-resonant heliocentric loitering
orbits** between Earth and Mars where transit vehicles top off consumables,
exchange/augment payload, and optionally serve as bent-pipe communication
relays during Sun-superior conjunctions. Key parameters:

- **WP heliocentric semi-major axis ≈ 1.196 AU**, giving an Earth-referenced
  synodic period exactly 2 (i.e. WP period = τ/2 where Mars's synodic period
  τ ≈ 26 months).
- **Availability cycle 2τ = 52 months = 4.27 years** per WP.
- **Four WPs in a constellation** (two phasings each at λ_31 family
  -156.6°/-116.6° and at λ_33 family -93.8°/-63.8°) cover **all** Earth-Mars
  opposition seasons (Leg-AB and Leg-CD interleaved over 2031, 2033, 2035,
  ..., 2046).
- **Transit structure:** Earth-to-Mars splits into Leg-A (Earth→WP) + WP
  loiter (5 day minimum, up to 145-180 d) + Leg-B (WP→Mars); reverse for
  return. Optimality metric Σv∞ = sum of four hyperbolic asymptote speeds.
- **Lambert grid scan** ("2-Leg Lambert", 5-day granularity) over thousands
  of leg pairs, NHATS-comparable methodology but without imposed round-trip
  Earth-start.

Quantitative results (Tables, pages 11-15):

- Min Σv∞ values per opposition season 2031-2046 are tabulated for both
  Leg-AB and Leg-CD; the 2-leg architecture sometimes beats nonstop and
  sometimes does not. Net 15-year deliverable mass: **Leg-AB delivers
  1,282,696 kg to Mars vs nonstop's 1,276,558 kg** (~0.5% improvement, not
  large — the win is at the per-opposition seasonal level, not the
  aggregate).
- **Reference vehicle:** Starship-class. m_i = 100,000 kg, m_X = 1,300,000 kg,
  I_sp = 380 s (v_EX = 3.726546 km/s). Earth parking 185 km, Mars parking
  382 km.
- **NEA-supplied WP construction:** worked example uses NEA 1996 XB27 (D =
  100-245 m, semi-major axis just outside the 1.196 AU WP, leads by 170 Mkm
  in 2031 increasing < 7 Mkm/yr). Outbound transit 445 d, return 520 d,
  roundtrip 3.3 yr. 493 catalogued NEAs orbit between Earth and Mars as of
  2025-03-12.
