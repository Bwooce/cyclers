## What Phase 1 does NOT deliver

By design:

* **Family continuation along POL1** -- Phase 2 will add natural-parameter or
  pseudo-arclength continuation in the POL1 branch, plus a mu_sun continuation
  path from the CR3BP limit (POL1's CR3BP analogue is the L1 Lyapunov orbit at
  the same x, py-derived-vy seed). The Phase 1 corrector is the building block.
* **Halo-BCR4BP extension** -- Phase 3. The Gate 3 weak-perturbation test shows
  the corrector handles 3D halos at small mu_sun; the strict-periodic halo
  family at the Andreu mu_sun requires period commensurability bookkeeping
  threaded through a 3D continuation.
* **KNOWN_CORPUS widening** -- Phase 4. The CandidateSignature / literature
  flagger does not yet accept BCR4BP-model fingerprints.
* **V-pipeline adaptation / catalogue admission** -- Phase 5. The V-pipeline
  assumes CR3BP / DE440-ephemeris models; BCR4BP needs a separate adapter and
  acceptance-tier definitions (BCR4BP-V0 etc.) before any catalogue writeback.
* **The coherent QBCP** -- the eight alpha_i Fourier functions of Andreu's
  full QBCP are NOT digested. Per the 2026-06-14 digest, harvesting them from
  Gimeno-Jorba 2018 Table 4 is a documented future step. Phase 1 implements
  the standard incoherent BCR4BP (the four-constant model), which is one
  fidelity rung above CR3BP and is correctly delivered.

## Sourced golden verdicts

### CR3BP-limit anchor (Gate 1 of test_bcr4bp_genome.py)

* **Pass.** Halo seed `(0.824024728136525, 0, -0.054501847320725, 0,
  0.164671964079122, 0)` at T = 2.7549 (Howell 1984 / NASA TN D-1949 family,
  sourced in `tests/search/test_cr3bp_periodic.py`) converges in the BCR4BP
  corrector at mu_sun = 0 with corrector residual < 1e-10, independent Radau
  closure < 1e-6, AND the converged orbit also closes under the CR3BP
  propagator (the two integrators are identical at mu_sun = 0; verified at
  the propagator level in `test_cr3bp_limit_propagator`).

### POL1 closure (Gate 2)

* **Pass.** Rosales-Jorba (2023) Table 4 POL1 IC (interpreted as canonical
  momentum `py`, converted to velocity `vy = py - x` per the CR3BP-limit
  Hamiltonian convention) seeded into the corrector with T held FIXED at the
  Sun-commensurate period `2 * pi / omega_sun` (n=1, ~6.79 TU) and free vars
  `(x, vy)`. Convergence achieved in 9 Newton iterations:
  * Corrector residual: 4.5e-14 (machine precision).
  * Independent Radau closure: 5.5e-11 (machine precision).
  * Sun-phase drift: 0 (T held exactly at the commensurate value).
  * Converged IC: `x = -0.844, vy = -0.022` (vs seed `x = -0.8369, vy =
    -0.0022`). The orbit stays in the L1 substitute neighbourhood (topology
    gate passes).

  The converged IC is NEAR but NOT AT the published POL1 -- expected, because
  POL1 is the QBCP dynamical substitute and the implemented model is the
  standard (incoherent) BCR4BP. Per `feedback_golden_tests_sourced_only`: the
  published value is the SEED, not the EXPECTED side of an equality assertion.
  The test asserts CLOSURE (both corrector and independent Radau) and
  TOPOLOGY (x stays in the L1 region), which both hold.

### POL2 closure (deliberately omitted)

* **Negative result, declared.** The Rosales-Jorba Table 4 POL2 IC (L2
  substitute) did not converge to a topologically-faithful BCR4BP L2 analogue
  with the symmetric (`free_vars = (x, vy)`, residual at T/2) pattern that
  worked for POL1; the Newton iteration either stalled or wandered to an
  unrelated orbit at `x = -4.92`. The L2 region in the BCR4BP at the Andreu
  mu_sun appears more sensitive than L1 (consistent with the dynamical-
  systems literature on QBCP L1 vs L2 substitute stability).
* **Not a Phase 1 blocker.** Phase 1's job is to deliver the substrate
  (corrector that finds SOMETHING at a published seed when one exists, and
  flags structured failure when one does not). The POL1 substitute landing is
  the sourced-anchor evidence the substrate works; POL2 falls into the
  "structured failure" category and is a Phase 2 family-continuation problem
  (start from POL1 and continue in some natural parameter to reach POL2).

## Phase 2 path of least resistance

**Recommendation: mu_sun continuation from CR3BP L1 Lyapunov toward the
Andreu BCR4BP POL1 substitute.** Rationale:

* The CR3BP L1 Lyapunov family at the Andreu mu (0.012150) at C_J near the L1
  energy is well-known and well-corrected by existing infrastructure
  (`cr3bp_general_periodic`).
* The mu_sun continuation parameter is single-dimensional and physically
  monotonic from 0 (CR3BP limit) to 328900.5423 (Andreu value).
* At mu_sun = 0 the family member closure is exact (verified Gate 1).
* The Phase 1 BCR4BPSystem dataclass takes mu_sun as a constructor argument
  -- no API changes needed.
* Expected payoff: a continuous family of "L1 dynamical substitutes" between
  the CR3BP Lyapunov and the BCR4BP-at-Andreu POL1 analogue; for cyclers
  research, this gives a parameterised one-rung-above-CR3BP probe of the
  Sun-perturbation sensitivity of Aldrin-style and S/L resonant-cycler
  candidates that currently sit at V1 (CR3BP) or V3 (DE440 ephemeris) but
  have no intermediate evidence.
* Alternative continuation parameters considered: x0 along the POL1 branch
  at fixed mu_sun (gives a POL1 family at the Andreu mu_sun, useful for
  cyclers that resonance-lock to the Sun synodic period), period T sliding
  off the commensurate value (gives invariant curves / quasi-periodic
  members, more complex bookkeeping). Mu_sun is the natural first axis.
