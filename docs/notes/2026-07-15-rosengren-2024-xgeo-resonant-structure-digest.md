## Scope

The most applied/motivational paper of this 4-paper cluster — framed around **space domain awareness
(SDA) beyond GEO ("xGEO")**, not a new dynamical result of its own. Defines xGEO rigorously as the
critical distance at which secular lunisolar-perturbation effects exceed Earth-oblateness effects.
Surveys the taxonomy of cislunar dynamical models: perturbed two-body (circumterrestrial/circumlunar
orbits) vs. the full gravitational N-body problem for everything else — lunar transfers, libration-
point orbits, **Earth-Moon cyclers**, mean-motion resonances, and other periodic/non-periodic exotic
orbits. Cites the group's own MMR/heteroclinic work (the three papers above) as the substantive
dynamical content underlying the SDA argument.

## Relevance to this project

Lower direct relevance than the three companions — this is a survey/motivation piece for an SDA
audience, not a new method or dataset. Worth keeping filed because it **explicitly classifies
Earth-Moon cyclers as one of the xGEO dynamical regimes** its group studies, and may be a useful
citation if the project ever needs to frame cislunar-cycler work in a domain-awareness context.
