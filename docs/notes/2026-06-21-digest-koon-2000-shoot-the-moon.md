# Digest: Shoot the Moon (2000)

## Metadata
* **Authors**: Wang Sang Koon, Martin W. Lo, Jerrold E. Marsden, Shane D. Ross
* **Date**: 2000
* **Title**: Shoot the Moon
* **Type**: Paper (AAS 00-166)
* **File**: `papers/koon-lo-marsden-ross-2000-shoot-the-moon.pdf`

## Key Concepts
* Application of dynamical systems theory to construct a low-energy transfer trajectory from the Earth to a ballistic capture at the Moon, similar to the 1991 Japanese Hiten mission.
* Validates Belbruno and Miller's Weak Stability Boundary (WSB) theory, explaining it via the invariant manifold structure of the Lagrange points of the Sun-Earth and Earth-Moon systems.
* Decouples the 4-body problem (Sun-Earth-Moon-Spacecraft) into two 3-body problems (Sun-Earth and Earth-Moon).
* Uses the unstable manifolds of periodic orbits about the Sun-Earth Lagrange points to provide a low energy transfer from Earth to the stable manifolds of periodic orbits around the Earth-Moon Lagrange points, resulting in ballistic capture.
* Integrates the final trajectory in the Bi-Circular Problem (BiCP) and verifies it using JPL's LTool with full ephemeris.

## Relevance to Cyclers
* Outlines the foundational methodology for coupling multiple restricted three-body problems to find heteroclinic/homoclinic transfers, which directly applies to finding multi-moon resonant tours and cyclers.
