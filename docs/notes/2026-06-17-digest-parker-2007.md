## What the paper actually is

A 342-page dynamical-systems treatment of **Earth-Moon Ballistic Lunar
Transfers (BLTs)** — low-energy single-maneuver transfers from a 185 km
LEO parking orbit to a lunar three-body orbit (typically a lunar Halo,
DPO, or L1/L2 Lyapunov orbit), via either the Sun-Earth manifold pathway
(Belbruno/Hiten-style weak-stability-boundary transfers extended into
3D) or direct two-maneuver halo transfers (the performance baseline).

The thesis's main contribution is a **systematic method** for building
3D BLTs using a six-parameter description (Sun-Earth-Moon angle θ,
manifold parameter τ, etc.) and the **BLT State Space Map** — a
parameter-space visualisation tool for identifying families of BLTs
(p. 339, §8.2.4). Key headline numbers from the abstract (p. iii) and
§8.2.4 (p. 340):

- Total ΔV as low as **3.2 km/s** (Trans-Lunar Injection)
  in the Patched Three-Body model, with **fewer than 120 days**
  transfer time.
- Under specific conditions: **< 3.2 km/s and < 100 days**.
- Departure opportunities exist from nearly any LEO altitude /
  inclination combination, nearly any day of the month.
- 25-40% payload-mass increase vs. conventional direct transfers
  (Hohmann-like fast transfers) for the same launch vehicle (abstract;
  also §7.1.1 pp. 291-301).
- Sun-Earth-Moon-spacecraft four-body system simplified to a
  **Patched Three-Body Model** (Sun-Earth CR3BP + Earth-Moon CR3BP
  joined via a Three-Body Sphere of Influence), validated against the
  JPL ephemeris in Chapter 5.

The body has eight chapters (Introduction, Models, Methodology, Direct
BLTs, Low-Energy BLTs, Families of BLTs, Practical Mission Design,
Summary). Appendices A-C catalogue **planar and 3D simple periodic
symmetric orbits in the Earth-Moon CR3BP** by symmetry class (Class 1
about L3, Class 2 about M1, Class 3 about M1+M2, etc.), with example
orbits and families documented (pp. 358-411).

## Catalogue / KNOWN_CORPUS relevance

### Earth-Moon cycler / cislunar BCT relevance

The thesis uses the word "cycler" twice (full-text grep), and neither
matches our catalogue definition:

1. **§7.3 (p. 333), "Earth-Moon Cyclers":** Parker observes that every
   BLT he constructs in the Patched Three-Body model has a
   "symmetric ballistic Earth-return transfer associated with it"
   (p. 333), so a spacecraft can build a back-and-forth round-trip
   mission concept. This is a *mission concept* (round-trip BLT), **not
   a cycler in the Aldrin / SnLm sense** (no continuous trajectory, no
   passenger-vehicle taxi model, no repeated planetary encounters with
   a free-flying spacecraft).
2. **Appendix A.3.2 (p. 375), Class 3 retrograde family:** Parker
   describes a family of CR3BP periodic orbits centered about the
   Earth+Moon barycenter that, as a member parameter is varied, "begin
   to take shape as Earth-Moon cyclers" — i.e., orbits that make close
   flybys of both Earth and Moon (p. 375). This is a *naming convention
   for a CR3BP periodic-orbit family*, not a transport-mission cycler.

The thesis is therefore **off-scope** for the current catalogue
(strict-cycler / quasi-cycler / precursor / MGA-tour, schema v4.7
2026-06-15) and for KNOWN_CORPUS. It is a textbook-grade reference for
the methodology behind:

- Low-energy single-burn Earth-Moon transfers (Belbruno WSB / BCT in 3D)
- BLT Maps (six-parameter design space)
- Patched Three-Body Model (Sun-Earth CR3BP + Earth-Moon CR3BP joined
  at a Three-Body Sphere of Influence)

## Errata vs the pre-fire survey

The pre-fire Mars-cycler wave digest filed Parker 2007 as: *"PhD thesis
on low-energy ballistic lunar transfers (WSB / BCT / weak-stability
boundary). Relevant for #316 / cislunar BCT lit-pass. File-only;
reference resource for any cislunar BCT discussion."*

The deep-read **confirms** that verdict completely. Two refinements:

1. The thesis is more specifically about **3D BLTs using lunar
   three-body staging orbits** (Halo, DPO, L1/L2 Lyapunov), not just
   transfers to low lunar orbit. The 3D extension is its central
   novelty contribution.
2. The two "cycler" mentions (§7.3 round-trip-BLT concept;
   Appendix A.3.2 CR3BP periodic-orbit family naming) are both
   **false friends** for our catalogue scope. The work as a whole is
   solidly within the **cislunar BCT / weak-stability-boundary
   transfer / Hiten-genealogy** methodology family — useful as a #316
   reference, not as a cycler corpus entry.
