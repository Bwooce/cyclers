## (A) Aldrin — the #174 GMAT run targeted the WRONG FLYBY

### The prime suspect, confirmed

The #174 generator (`aldrin_row`) built a **Mars** return-flyby Target: seed v∞
8.88 km/s, required turn 93.0°, Achieve the outgoing v∞ vector at the Mars SOI. It
converged to **0.175 km/s**.

But Aldrin's documented per-synodic maintenance cost is the **Earth** return-flyby
turn deficit, NOT the Mars flyby:

- `2026-06-07-aldrin-continuation-v3-evidence.md` §2, lap 0: "turn req **93.0°** /
  turn max **68.5°** → **powered**" — this is the **Earth** (geocentric) flyby
  (`maintain.py` charges it at Earth; V∞_E ≈ 6.86 km/s in-family DE440).
- The #148 primer-refine finding (quoted in `2026-06-07-oberth-flyby-recost.md`):
  "the 2.9138 km/s is **entirely** an Earth-flyby turn-deficit charge, heliocentric
  coasts near-ballistic, so flyby geometry is the only lever."
- `core/flyby.dv_from_turn_deficit` docstring: McConaghy 2002 Table 4 gives the
  **Earth** δ_required = 84° vs δ_max = 72° (200 km Earth flyby).

So **0.175 km/s is the wrong maneuver** — the Mars flyby is cheap because the
documented constraint lives at Earth, not Mars. It is not comparable to the 2.9138
/ 1.9336 references. **(Wrong-leg confirmed.)**

### The corrected Earth-flyby GMAT run

`aldrin_earth_row` (#176) targets the **Earth** return flyby: V∞_E = 6.86 km/s,
required turn 93.0°, central body Earth, BC at Earth periapsis. Two corrections
were needed to make it physical:

1. **Subsurface-seed clamp.** The Jones Eq.2 periapsis for a 93° turn at 6.86 km/s
   is **rp = 3207 km — 3171 km BELOW the Earth's surface**. Seeding at that B let
   the DC "pass through the planet" and recover a spuriously cheap 0.362 km/s (the
   same artifact class as the Mars 0.175). `clamp_safe_rp` re-seeds at the
   **safe-periapsis** impact parameter (B = 12 559 km, rp = 6678 km / 300 km alt),
   whose natural hyperbola turns by the cone max 68.0°; the DC's TCM then pays the
   honest **25° turn deficit** at a real flyby.

2. **The single-impulse periapsis maneuver is expensive.** With the safe-rp seed,
   GMAT (DC, 6 it.) converges to **3.287 km/s** — ABOVE the asymptote 2.97. A
   single 3-DOF impulse at periapsis rotates the *periapsis* velocity
   (v_p = √(V∞²+2μ/rp) ≈ 12.9 km/s), and rotating a fast vector is costly.

### Reconciling the four numbers (numerically reproduced, #176)

For the Earth flyby (V∞_E 6.86 km/s, rp_safe 6678 km, deficit 25.0°):

| maneuver strategy | ΔV (km/s) | source |
|---|---|---|
| single impulse rotating **v_p** by the deficit | 5.586 | analytic (`#176`) |
| **GMAT single-impulse periapsis TCM (3-DOF)** | **3.287** | **GMAT R2022a, DC 6 it.** |
| GMAT two-burn DC (any feasible point) | 3.640 | GMAT (DC, not optimal) |
| GMAT two-burn Yukon-MINIMIZED | 3.548 | GMAT (Yukon NLP, 116 it.) |
| **asymptote rotation** (`dv_from_turn_deficit`) | **2.970** | analytic ≈ ref **2.9138** |
| **Oberth widen-cone-restore** (2 tangential) | **1.971** | analytic ≈ ref **1.9336** |

The spread is entirely about **which maneuver strategy is allowed**:

- **Asymptote 2.97 (≈ ref 2.9138):** rotate the V∞ asymptote at infinity. The
  conservative no-Oberth model the catalogue currently cites.
- **Oberth 1.97 (≈ ref 1.9336):** the deep-well-optimal *two-tangential-burn*
  widen-cone-then-restore (slow down → wider cone delivers the full 93° → speed
  back up). **Dual-method confirmed** by the analytic formula AND a direct two-body
  integration to **1 part in 1e9** (`2026-06-07-oberth-independent-confirmation.md`
  #154). It is a *continuous-tangential* strategy.
- **GMAT impulsive (3.29–3.64):** GMAT's `ImpulsiveBurn` maneuvers at periapsis
  (single DC, two-burn DC, AND Yukon-minimized two-burn) all land in **3.3–3.6
  km/s — near the asymptote, NOT the Oberth value.** The widen-restore credit is a
  specific staged-tangential geometry that a small set of impulsive periapsis burns
  does not realize; the Yukon optimizer converged (116 it., "Optimizer Converged")
  to a 3.55 km/s local optimum for the impulsive-at-periapsis parameterization, not
  the 1.97 continuous-tangential optimum.

### Honest reading (NOT tuned)

The corrected GMAT did **not** reproduce 1.93 as the task hypothesised ("GMAT also
burns at periapsis with Oberth credit, so it SHOULD land near 1.93"). That
hypothesis was wrong for **impulsive** GMAT maneuvers: a single (or few) impulsive
periapsis burn does NOT capture the Oberth turn credit for a *pure equal-|V∞|
turn* — exactly the boundary `2026-06-07-oberth-flyby-recost.md` flagged ("neither
published formula gives an Oberth credit for a pure equal-|V∞| turn deficit; both
maneuver at the SOI for the deficit case"). The Oberth 1.97 is OUR distinct
widen-restore construction, integration-confirmed but **not** what a GMAT impulsive
flyby pays.

**What GMAT independently establishes:** an impulsive Earth-flyby maneuver
achieving the documented 93° turn at a *safe* periapsis costs **~3.3 km/s** — it
brackets the asymptote 2.9138 from ABOVE, confirming 2.9138 is **not** an
over-estimate for the *impulsive* maneuver class (the #174 reading "2.9138 is
conservative" only holds against the continuous Oberth strategy, not against
GMAT's impulsive answer).

### Defensible operational figure (A)

A bound pair with explicit model provenance, NOT a single number:

- **Conservative / impulsive upper bound: ~2.97–3.3 km/s** — the asymptote-rotation
  2.9138 (catalogue) is the published-comparison value; GMAT's independent
  impulsive flyby confirms the impulsive maneuver class sits at ~3.3 km/s (slightly
  above, since GMAT rotates the fast periapsis velocity).
- **Oberth-optimal lower bound: 1.9336 km/s** — the continuous widen-restore
  strategy, dual-method confirmed (formula + integration, #154), the figure a real
  mission flying the deep-well-optimal maneuver would pay.

The asymptote 2.9138 stays the defensible **conservative** headline; the Oberth
1.9336 is the defensible **optimistic** lower bound. **GMAT's 0.175 (#174) must be
retired — it is the wrong leg (Mars, not Earth).**

## (B) S1L1 — 7.29 km/s is the forced-v∞-magnitude artifact; 62 m/s stands

### Why #174 produced 7.29 km/s

The #174 generator (`s1l1_row`, `vinf-vector` mode) made each of the 7 Mars flybys
an **independent** Target that re-anchors to its own App-C (v∞⁻, v∞⁺) pair and
**Achieves the outgoing v∞ VECTOR** (direction AND magnitude). Summed converged
TCM = **7.292 km/s** (reproduced: 1.671+0.401+2.157+0.598+0.334+1.273+0.858).

This forces two things a real cycler maintenance does NOT pay:

1. The **|v∞| magnitude jump** between consecutive App-C nodes (the v∞ "breathes
   3.2–8.0 km/s" across the 7 cycles) — a flyby cannot change |v∞| ballistically,
   so forcing it charges the full magnitude change as fuel.
2. **Manufactured large Mars turns.** Re-anchoring each node to its own App-C pair
   demands Mars turns of **58°, 48°, 8°, 40°, 58°, 37°, 94°** — far exceeding the
   Mars ballistic cone at safe periapsis (**26–64°**). Even *direction-only* (drop
   the magnitude force, charge only the Mars turn deficit) still sums to **~14
   km/s** (#176), because these large turns simply do not fit Mars's cone.

### Why the natural continuous flight is 62 m/s

The #169 continuous single-seed chain (REBOUND/IAS15, independent integrator +
ephemeris) does NOT re-anchor. It threads ONE trajectory through all 20 App-C
nodes, and at each **Mars** arrival the spacecraft's v∞ needs only a **small**
bend — `2026-06-08-s1l1-continuous-v4-results.md` Mars bends: **10.0°, 5.6°,
10.5°, 23.2°, 11.9°, 7.4°, 16.9°** — every one INSIDE the Mars cone (maxbend
18–64°), so every Mars flyby is **ballistic (0 m/s)**. The maintenance is charged
at the **Earth** flybys (whose cones are far wider, 68–108°) and totals **62 m/s
over 7 cycles** (22 m/s |v∞|-magnitude + 40 m/s one un-bendable Earth shortfall at
node 20). That is the natural cost of the cycler *as continuously flown*.

The large Mars turns in #174 are an **artifact of independent re-anchoring**: they
do not occur in the continuous flight. #174 mislocated the maintenance (forced it
onto Mars node-by-node) AND over-constrained it (forced |v∞|).

### The operational GMAT probe — and why it is ill-posed in GMAT

A `bplane-position` mode was added (#176): aim each flyby at the **next encounter's
B-plane POSITION** (BdotR, BdotT) only, letting |v∞| ride free. Running the 7
scripts gave **erratic** TCMs (0.1–12.3 km/s, Mars21 pathological), because GMAT's
B-plane variables read AFTER the periapsis burn are an *approach* (incoming-
asymptote) quantity — unstable/ill-defined post-periapsis. The B-plane-only target
is ALSO conceptually trivial in isolation: *any* ballistic hyperbola through a
chosen B-plane point costs zero ΔV (you are just selecting the approach geometry),
so a per-flyby B-plane-only target is not the right instrument for the chain
maintenance. The faithful operational measure is the **continuous chain** (#169),
which charges exactly the flyby-uncreditable residual (|v∞| magnitude + un-bendable
turn) and nothing else.

### Verdict (B): the S1L1 V3-ballistic basis STANDS (with its existing caveat)

- **62 m/s is the honest operational maintenance** in S1L1's defining patched-conic
  model, independently reproduced by REBOUND/IAS15 (#169). It STANDS.
- **7.29 km/s is an over-constrained artifact** (forced node-to-node |v∞| + Mars-
  located turns that don't occur in continuous flight). It should NOT be cited as
  the S1L1 maintenance ΔV.
- The V3 already carries the correct caveat (`#169`): the 62 m/s holds in the
  patched-conic model; a fully-continuous **Mars-perturbed** flight needs active
  per-flyby maintenance whose magnitude #169 could not bound (the post-Mars legs
  diverge). That caveat is unchanged by this reconciliation — it does NOT need
  *new* qualifying. The 7.29 km/s does **not** justify qualifying the V3 because it
  measures a different (over-constrained, mislocated) quantity, not the maintenance.

**S1L1 V3-ballistic basis: STANDS. No new qualification required.** The standing
patched-conic-only caveat remains the honest scope statement.
