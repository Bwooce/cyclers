## 2. What the paper actually is

A **JPL Voyager-precursor mission-design feasibility paper**, written under contract NAS 7-100 to survey "Grand Tour" outer-planet opportunities and identify their constraining and competing factors. The paper is explicitly described as work in the **formative stage** of JPL's outer-planet exploration program (§I p. 1): "This paper is therefore limited to a discussion of the possibilities and options and a discussion of the factors that influence the design choice."

The paper has six substantive sections (§I-VII):

1. **§I Introduction** (p. 1) — scope; preliminary discussion of JSP / JUN options.
2. **§II Background** (p. 1) — origin of the Grand Tour concept (Flandro 1966 four-planet JSUN; Kingsland's two-class JSUN analysis; subsequent migration to JSP and JUN to avoid Saturn ring hazards).
3. **§III Characteristics of the Opportunities** (pp. 2-4) — launch vehicle (Titan IIID/Centaur/Burner II), Table 1 mission table (JSUN, JSP, JUN, Pluto, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune options with C3, weight, ToF, flyby altitude per planet), Table 2 launch periods, general design charts (Figs 2-7).
4. **§IV Satellite Encounter Opportunities** (pp. 4-6) — Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus satellite-encounter possibilities; details of JSP '76 mission.
5. **§V Mission Trajectory Parameters** (pp. 6-7) — JSP '76 / JSP '77 / JUN '79 illustrations; Figs 11-20 (spacecraft-view sketches of Jupiter, Saturn, Galilean moons).
6. **§VI Navigation** (pp. 7-8) — navigation accuracy floor (~5 arcsec optical guidance, ~200-1000 km aiming accuracy), ΔV budget for trajectory corrections (160-250 m/s).
7. **§VII Expected Future Developments** (p. 9) — speculative.

Eleven citations (Refs 1-9 on p. 9), of which the most relevant successors are Flandro 1966, Kingsland 1969, Wallace 1968, Ball-Duxbury 1970, and Bourke-Beerer 1970 (Mariner Venus/Mercury preliminary design — relevant to paper 2 of this digest set).

**Critically: this paper precedes the Mariner Jupiter/Saturn (MJS) program that became Voyager 1+2.** Voyager's flight design (1972 selection, 1977 launch) is not in this paper; the paper describes a JSP-or-JUN selection that was later folded back into the JSUN flyby trajectory Flandro had analyzed in 1966 once the Pioneer 10/11 radiation-environment results came back. The paper does mention this contingency on p. 9: "If this environment is found to be so severe that it will permanently damage a spacecraft passing through it, it may be necessary to use Grand Tour opportunities for which the spacecraft passes outside the radiation belts. This could cause the launches to be shifted to later years."

### 3.2 V_∞ per encounter — **NOT PUBLISHED**

**Critical finding:** Table 1 publishes **C3 at launch, ToF, and flyby altitudes** but does **NOT publish per-encounter V_∞**. The paper's tradeoff curves (Figs 2-7) plot **injected weight, arrival date, and flyby altitude (in planetary radii)** as functions of mission duration — again no V_∞ at intermediate planets. The bending-angle equation (p. 8) is symbolic only: `γ = 180 - 2 tan⁻¹(b·V_∞²/μ)` without numerical V_∞ values for any specific flyby.

The only V_∞-related quantity that appears is implicit in the Jupiter flyby radius/altitude tradeoffs (Figs 2-7 hatched bands showing the radius achievable as a function of ToF and launch period), and the §VI navigation discussion ("a Callisto flyby at 100,000 km perpendicular to its orbit requires 11 m/s correction for 100% mass error" — implies V_∞ at Callisto but not at the heliocentric encounter with Jupiter).

### 3.3 JSP '76 detailed parameters (§V p. 6)

This is the only mission for which detailed numerical trajectory parameters are given:

- **Jupiter arrival date:** December 27.25, 1977 (sourced footnote p. 6: "If a 21-day launch period is chosen, then the maximum energy requirement will be a C₃ of 115 km²/sec². The center of the launch period occurs on July 30, 1976, requiring a C₃ of 108.7.")
- **Jupiter closest-approach altitude:** 3800 km at the launch-period start; 3470 km at the end.
- **Galilean satellite closest-approach distances:** Io 158,000 km; Europa 275,000 km; Ganymede 158,000 km; Callisto 228,000 km.
- **Saturn arrival (JSP '76):** January 16, 1980 ("For this trajectory, encounters with the two satellites Dione and Tethys occur with closest approach distances of 173,000 km and 210,000 km").
- **Saturn periapsis altitude:** 333,700 km (Saturn 18:53 view in Fig 17, distance 2,000,000 km).

Even this detailed mission has **no V_∞ at Jupiter** or **V_∞ at Saturn** sourced numerically. The figures all show heliocentric / planet-centric views without V_∞ tabulation.

### 3.4 Navigation ΔV (§VI pp. 7-8)

- Fuel loading: 5-8% spacecraft weight for JSP missions; 15% for JUN '79.
- For 500-km aiming accuracy: 160 m/s ΔV budget.
- For 300-km aiming accuracy: 1000 m/s for JSP (and Uranus on JUN) → 250 m/s fuel loading.
- Optical guidance system: 5 arcsec celestial direction uncertainty (1σ).
- Combined aiming accuracy: 200-1000 km at the outer planets.

These are spacecraft-design budgets, NOT per-leg trajectory deterministic ΔVs.

### 3.5 Did this become Voyager 1+2?

The 1976 JSUN (interior, inside-Saturn-rings) opportunity at 561 d to Jupiter, 1363 d to Saturn, 2798 d to Uranus, 3894 d to Neptune was the precursor to the **JSUN concept**. Voyager 1 and 2 launched in 1977, and Voyager 2 reached Neptune in 1989 (~4400 d). **Voyager 2's actual flown trajectory differs substantially from the 1976 JSUN profile in this paper:**

- This paper's 1976 JSUN reaches Neptune in 3894 d = 10.7 yr.
- Voyager 2 launched 1977-08-20 and reached Neptune 1989-08-25 (12.01 yr ≈ 4388 d).
- Per Voyager flight-design lore (Stone-Lane 1979 and later), the actual flight trajectory used the 1977 launch window because the Mariner Jupiter/Saturn (MJS) program was approved later than this paper anticipated, and the 1977 windows (Table 1 rows 2-3) were within reach.

The 1977 JSUN (interior) at C3=120 is the closest table row to Voyager 2's actual baseline. ToF is 511 d to Jupiter (Voyager 2 actual: 1979-07-09 = 689 d, longer because Voyager 2 used a slower trajectory), 1095 d to Saturn (Voyager 2 actual: 1981-08-25 = 1466 d), 2342 d to Uranus (Voyager 2 actual: 1986-01-24 = 3079 d), 3372 d to Neptune (Voyager 2 actual: 4388 d).

**Conclusion: this paper's tables are the 1971 Grand Tour design baseline — superseded by the actual MJS/Voyager flight design (Stone-Lane 1979 / Kohlhase 1989 / Diehl-Kaplan 1985 etc.), which we do NOT have in cyclers_pdf at this commit.**

## 4. Catalogue admission verdict

### 4.1 V0 admissibility test

Per the Heaton-Longuski 2003 U00-01 admission precedent and the Wolf-Smith 1995 Cassini negative-verdict precedent (`docs/notes/2026-06-17-digest-wolf-smith-1995-cassini.md` §4.1), the V0 evidence standard for an `mga_tour` row is:

**Per-flyby epoch + V_∞ tuple + body sequence are minimum.**

Bourke 1971 publishes:

- Per-flyby epoch: PARTIAL. Arrival **dates** are given for JSP '76 to-the-day at Jupiter (1977-12-27.25) and Saturn (1980-01-16). For other missions in Table 1, only the **launch year window** is given (e.g. "1976 JSUN"), not the specific launch date. ToFs to each planet are sourced per Table 1 in days, which gives arrival epochs derivable from launch date — but the launch date itself is just "center of the 15-day launch period in YYYY".
- Body sequence: YES for JSP '76 (E → J → S → P with Galilean and Saturnian moon flybys distances), JSP '77, JSUN missions (E → J → S → U → N), JUN missions (E → J → U → N). All are explicitly named in Table 1 headers.
- V_∞ tuple: **NO**. Table 1 is a launch-energy / flyby-altitude / ToF table. There is no V_∞ column anywhere in the paper. The bending equation is symbolic only.

**Verdict: the paper is ONE COLUMN SHORT of the V0 `mga_tour` minimum** (same shortfall as Wolf-Smith 1995 Cassini).

### 4.2 Two layered problems

1. **V_∞ absent.** Same blocker as Wolf-Smith 1995. The V_∞ data could in principle be back-derived from STOUR / patched-conic reproduction (Kingsland 1969 STOUR is referenced and would produce them), but that is **derived, not sourced** — violates the [`golden-tests sourced-only`](MEMORY.md) rule.

2. **These are 1971 design baselines, NOT the flown Voyager 1+2 trajectories.** The brief asks whether this paper matches Voyager 1+2's actual flight. **It does not.** The Voyager flight design was finalized in 1973-1977 by the MJS team (Stone et al., Kohlhase et al., Diehl et al.) — none of those references are in cyclers_pdf at commit `fa38aae` for this admission cycle. If the catalogue is to admit "Voyager 1+2 mga_tour" rows, the correct sources are the **post-flight reconstruction** papers (Stone 1977/1979 Science articles; Kohlhase 1989 "Voyager Mission Description"; the JPL D-document series), NOT this 1971 paper.

### 4.3 Recommendation

**RECOMMEND: do NOT admit Bourke-Friedman-Penzo-Stavro 1971 as a `mga_tour` row in the catalogue.** Two reasons:

1. **V_∞ data not published** (V0 minimum fails, same as Wolf-Smith 1995).
2. **This is the 1971 pre-MJS/pre-Voyager Grand Tour design, NOT the flown Voyager 1+2 trajectory.** Admitting it as a Voyager catalogue row would be a factual error: the Grand Tour as designed in 1971 became JSP and JUN missions, and the JSUN four-planet single-spacecraft baseline was DESCOPED in the early-1970s funding cuts before being partially restored as Voyager 2's extended mission. Voyager 1+2 as flown is a separate trajectory family that was not finalized when this paper was written.

Instead:

1. **File Bourke 1971 as a KNOWN_CORPUS literature anchor** for the JSP / JUN / JSUN design heritage. This is the canonical Voyager-precursor design study and an important historical reference, but it does not have to be a catalogue row.

## 6. Errata

Versus the task brief and the paper text:

- **Brief said "Voyager precursor — extract per-encounter V_∞ tables for the Grand Tour designs that preceded Voyager 1+2's actual flight design."** The paper publishes C3, ToF, and flyby altitudes — but **no V_∞ tables anywhere**. The brief overestimated the paper's data content.
- **Brief said "Check whether the Grand Tour studied in this 1971 paper matches the actual Voyager 1+2 flown trajectories (1977-1989), or differs (e.g. Stone 1977 / outer-planet design may have updated the 1971 baseline)."** **They differ significantly.** Voyager 1 (J+S only) and Voyager 2 (JSUN) trajectories were not finalized until 1973-1977 by the MJS team. The 1971 paper's Table 1 1977 JSUN row at C3=120 km²/s² is the closest match to Voyager 2's actual launch, but ToF figures differ by 200-1000 d at each planet, indicating a different launch geometry / Jupiter swingby altitude.
- **Paper-internal cross-references** are mostly clean; the references list is complete (Refs 1-9) and the figure callouts in §IV-V check out against the Figures 2-20 pages.
- **Author affiliation footnote** (** p. 1) "Group supervisor" applies only to Bourke; the three "†" footnote co-authors are technical staff (Friedman, Penzo, Stavro).
