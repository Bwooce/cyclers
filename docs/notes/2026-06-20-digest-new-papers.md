# Digest: New Earth-Mars Cycler Papers (2026-06-20)

This digest processes the batch of papers acquired on 2026-06-20.

## 1. machado-2020.pdf
* **Title:** Parametric Design of a Crew Transfer Vehicle for Earth–Mars Cyclers
* **Digest:** Presents a parametric design model for a crew transfer vehicle designed to ferry humans between planets and a cycling spacecraft. Estimates dry/total mass based on crew size, mission duration, planetary V-infinities, and braking strategies (propulsive vs aerodynamic). Validates feasibility within current launch limits.
* **Verdict:** **Digested** (Out-of-scope for CR3BP trajectory extraction, but provides key operational limits/context for cycler feasibility).

## 2. vergaaij-2018-time-optimal-solar-sail-heteroclinic-connections.pdf
* **Title:** Time-optimal solar sail heteroclinic-like connections for an Earth-Mars cycler
* **Digest:** Investigates using continuous low-thrust (solar sails) to traverse heteroclinic-like connections between the L1/L2 libration points of the Earth-Sun and Mars-Sun systems. Time-optimal framework.
* **Verdict:** **Digested** (Advanced dynamics continuous-thrust application, complementary to our ballistic patched-conic/CR3BP catalogue).

## 3. russell-2006-systematic-method-design-earth-mars-cyclers.pdf
* **Title:** Optimization of a Broad Class of Ephemeris Model Earth-Mars Cyclers
* **Digest:** Develops a continuation algorithm to transition idealized circular-coplanar parent cycler solutions into highly accurate finite-duration ephemeris solutions over 7 full cycles. Applied to 203 parent cyclers (including the Aldrin cycler), finding 9 parent cyclers capable of < 1m/s total maneuver requirements, and establishing practical feasibility for ephemeris cyclers.
* **Verdict:** **Digested** (A crucial bridge methodology from theoretical to flown cyclers; S/L naming conventions apply).

## 4. chen-russell-ocampo-2002-earth-mars-cyclers-free-return.pdf
* **Title:** A Low-Thrust Version of the Aldrin Cycler
* **Digest:** Seeks a low-thrust version of the Aldrin cycler to reduce the infeasibly large hyperbolic excess velocities (V_inf) at the planetary encounters, especially at Mars, in exchange for cycler propellant expenditure. Shows trade studies over 7 synodic periods minimizing total system mass.
* **Verdict:** **Digested** (Powered cycler category; provides comparison metrics for ballistic trajectories).

## 5. chen-russell-ocampo-2005-earth-mars-cyclers-multiple-impulse-free-return.pdf
* **Title:** Powered Earth-Mars Cycler with Three-Synodic-Period Repeat Time
* **Digest:** Constructs a cycler with acceptable transfer times and moderate encounter velocities by patching 3-synodic-period semicyclers using high-efficiency low-thrust propulsion. Reduces required cycler vehicle count to only three (instead of four+ for unpowered ballistic).
* **Verdict:** **Digested** (Powered cycler extension).

## 6. chen-2002-earth-mars-cyclers.pdf / chen-2012.pdf
* **Title:** Earth-Mars Cyclers (and associated low-thrust / multiple-impulse variations)
* **Digest:** K.J. Chen's thesis/extended works forming the foundation of the powered and low-thrust variants of the Aldrin and 3-synodic cyclers described above. 
* **Verdict:** **Digested** (Corroborating theoretical background for powered cyclers).
