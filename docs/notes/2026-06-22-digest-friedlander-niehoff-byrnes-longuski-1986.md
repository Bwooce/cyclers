# Digest — Friedlander, Niehoff, Byrnes & Longuski 1986, "Circulating Transportation Orbits Between Earth and Mars"

**Acquired 2026-06-22** (user-supplied PDF; the long-standing #116 / #416 acquisition
gap). JPL + SAIC. The **canonical early cycler paper** — predates Russell entirely
and is the primary source for the **VISIT** circulating-orbit family (Niehoff) and a
co-canonical source (with Aldrin) for the **Up/Down Escalator**. 13 pp, text-layer.
Filed `cyclers_pdf/papers/friedlander-niehoff-byrnes-longuski-1986-circulating-transportation-orbits-earth-mars.pdf`;
CORPUS_INDEX registered.

Purpose here: the genuinely-independent (1986, pre-Russell) primary for the #415/#416
ΔV bands — does it corroborate the near-ballistic concept and the powered band? **Yes,
both.** All numbers with line/section citations from the text layer.

## Near-ballistic class — the VISIT orbits (Niehoff)

- **VISIT-1:** period **1.25 yr**, perihelion ≈ **0.94 AU**, aphelion ≈ **1.39 AU**.
- **VISIT-2:** period **1.5 yr** (more frequent encounters, slightly higher speeds).
- **The load-bearing quote:** VISIT-1 is *"a 'free orbit' meaning that no midcourse
  ΔV is required other than navigation targeting maneuvers which will be necessary to
  compensate for expected small errors."* Perihelion/aphelion *"maintained nearly
  constant"*; **encounter speeds fairly constant 4.2–4.5 km/s** (low V∞ — unlike the
  high-V∞ Aldrin escalator).
- BASIS / CLASS: **ballistic / near-ballistic (TR ≥ 1)** — deterministic maintenance
  ΔV ≈ 0 (only navigation), stated qualitatively (no m/s tier census). MODEL: 20-yr
  propagation (real planetary geometry; the paper checks the orbit holds over 20 yr).

## Powered class — the Up/Down Escalator (Aldrin-type)

- Circular-coplanar: *"A relatively small maneuver of about **230 m/sec** performed at
  aphelion of the Escalator orbit"* (per-orbit, to rotate the line of apsides).
- Real 15-yr cycle (Table 3, Up Escalator): *"the optimal trajectory covering this 15
  year cycle requires propulsive maneuvers on only **three of the seven orbits**...
  the sum of these three maneuvers is **approximately seven times the per-orbit
  requirement** in the circular coplanar problem"* (≈ 7 × 230 m/s ≈ **1.6 km/s / 15
  yr**). Maneuvers near aphelion (~8 months from Mars encounter). High encounter
  speeds (Escalator V∞ much larger than VISIT).
- CLASS: **powered (TR < 1)**. This is the same number Byrnes-Longuski-Aldrin 1993
  (1.73/2.04 km/s/15 yr) and Rauwolf 2002 (1.56/1.61 km/s/15 yr) later refined — and
  Byrnes & Longuski co-author all three, so this is the **origin** of the "3 of 7
  orbits, ~1.6 km/s/15 yr" powered-Aldrin figure.

## Band impact (folds into `2026-06-22-dv-band-definitions.md`, #415/#416)

- **Independent corroboration of the ballistic *concept*, from 1986:** the VISIT
  family establishes that **free (ballistic) Earth-Mars circulating orbits exist**,
  needing only navigation maneuvers, with low encounter speeds — a primary source
  predating Russell-Ocampo by ~18 years and independent of the Aldrin escalator line.
- **Caveat (honesty):** Byrnes & Longuski co-author this, Byrnes-Aldrin 1993, and
  McConaghy 2004 — so it is the same broad Purdue/JPL school, **not** orthogonal to
  the Longuski lineage. What it adds is (a) **earliest-date** independence from
  Russell's specific tier derivation, and (b) the **VISIT family** as a distinct
  ballistic family (Niehoff), separate from Aldrin.
- **It does NOT reproduce the < 1 / < 10 / < 300 m/s tiered census** — VISIT is
  described qualitatively as "free / navigation-only," not with a tiered m/s budget.
  So Russell-Ocampo 2006 remains the sole source of the *exact tier counts*; Friedlander
  1986 corroborates the *existence and floor* of the near-ballistic class.
- **Powered band:** now four independent-ish primaries for ~1.5–2 km/s/15 yr, 3-of-7
  orbits — Friedlander 1986 (origin), Byrnes-Aldrin 1993, Rauwolf 2002, McConaghy 2004.
