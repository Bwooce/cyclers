# Digest: Dynamical Systems, the Three-Body Problem, and Space Mission Design

## Metadata
* **Authors**: Wang Sang Koon, Martin W. Lo, Jerrold E. Marsden, Shane D. Ross
* **Date**: 2006
* **Title**: Dynamical Systems, the Three-Body Problem and Space Mission Design
* **Type**: Book
* **File**: `papers/koon-lo-marsden-ross-2006-dynamical-systems-mission-design.pdf`

## Key Concepts
* Comprehensive textbook on the application of dynamical systems theory (invariant manifolds, Poincare sections, heteroclinic connections) to the Circular Restricted Three-Body Problem (CR3BP).
* Extensive coverage of the L1 and L2 Lagrangian points, their Lyapunov and Halo orbit families, and the stable and unstable manifolds associated with them.
* Explores applications such as the Genesis mission trajectory design, lunar missions (e.g., "Shoot the Moon" transfer), and multi-moon orbiter tours in the Jovian system (Jovian tour).
* Formalizes the "tube dynamics" approach for patching together manifolds from different three-body problems to construct fuel-efficient interplanetary trajectories.
* Details the mathematical framework for the patched conic approximation vs. full CR3BP dynamics.

## Relevance to Cyclers
* The fundamental text for using invariant manifolds in mission design. The techniques described here for finding heteroclinic connections and using Poincare sections are the foundation of the methods used to find cycling trajectories and multi-body resonant tours.
