## What the V4 fallback adds over V3

V3 (IAS15; #331) propagates the spacecraft as a test particle under
planet-frame two-body Kepler about Uranus, with moons placed by
circular-coplanar Kepler ephemerides. V4 here adds:

1. **Uranus J2 zonal harmonic** — Jacobson 2014, AJ 148:76, Table 4:
   J2 = 3.34343e-3, R_eq = 25559 km. The dominant non-spherical-primary
   effect. Vallado Eq. 8-37 implementation. At Umbriel's SMA the J2
   perturbation is ~3e-5 of the central acceleration; at Oberon's, ~7e-6.
   Small per-step, cumulative over a ~30-day cycle.

2. **Classical Uranian moons as third-body perturbers** — Miranda, Ariel,
   Umbriel, Titania, Oberon. Battin Eq. 8.60 standard formulation with
   Hill-sphere softening at each moon's Hill radius (handles the
   patched-conic Lambert-endpoint singularity — the spacecraft is AT a
   moon at leg start/end by Lambert geometry, so the bare third-body
   formula has r_b - r_sc = 0 there).

3. **The Lambert-tour moons as real perturbers throughout the leg** —
   Umbriel and Oberon perturb the spacecraft during the legs, not just
   at the encounter points (V2/V3 only intersect them at rendezvous).

## What the V4 fallback does NOT add (and why)

- **Real (non-circular, non-coplanar) moon ephemerides via SPICE** — requires
  URA111/URA107 kernels which are unavailable. The circular-coplanar
  ephemeris is therefore retained. Adding J2 still tests whether the
  dominant V4-class perturbation breaks the V3 signature.
- **Solar third-body perturbation** — Uranus's heliocentric distance is ~19
  AU; the solar perturbation on a Uranus-system spacecraft is ~1e-9 km/s²
  at Oberon's distance — three orders of magnitude below the J2 term.
  Omitting it does not change the verdict materially. Including it
  requires an epoch + Uranus heliocentric ephemeris, which we also don't
  have without SPICE.
- **Uranus J3, J4** — Jacobson 2014 gives J3 essentially zero (Uranus is
  closely axisymmetric); J4 ~ -2.9e-5, two orders of magnitude below J2.
  Adequate for a V4-fallback verdict.

## Interpretation

The bounded near-5:1 Umbriel-Oberon synodic-resonance signature reported
by V2/V3 is **NOT** a Keplerian-coplanar idealization artifact. It survives
the introduction of:

- Uranus's J2 zonal harmonic (the dominant non-spherical-primary effect)
- five classical Uranian moons as third-body perturbers (including the
  tour moons themselves throughout the leg, plus Titania — the largest
  classical Uranian moon)

  analytic Lambert+Kepler chain

agreement ~1.1e4 – 1.5e4 km on V3 drifts of ~3e5 – 5e5 km — a 2-5%
relative deviation). This is the expected scale of V4-class perturbation
on a near-resonance moon-tour.
